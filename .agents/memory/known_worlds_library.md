# Aurora — Known Worlds Lore Library
**Built:** 2026-05-13
**Purpose:** Canon world data for the "Load a Known World" import system. All 8 worlds are designed to let players LIVE in them — the main story is a living backdrop, not a closed narrative. Players can intercept, rival, or surpass canon characters.

---

## FANTASY

### 1. Middle-earth (Lord of the Rings)
**Setting:** High Fantasy continent — vast lands including Shire, Rohan, Gondor, Mordor, Mirkwood, Rivendell, Lothlórien, Eriador, Harad, Rhûn
**Climate:** Diverse — temperate grasslands in Rohan and the Shire, arctic north (Forodwaith), volcanic wastelands of Mordor, ancient forests, icy mountain passes
**Magic Level:** Low-Medium — magic is rare, woven into the world's fabric. Only Maiar (Gandalf, Saruman, Sauron) wield obvious power. Elves have subtle, ancient enchantments. Most humans see magic as myth.
**Technology Level:** Medieval — swords, bows, siege weapons. Dwarven engineering (Moria), Númenórean craftsmanship. No gunpowder, no machines.
**Danger Level:** High — war is imminent. Orcs, Ringwraiths, Balrogs, Shelob, Wargs, Barrow-wights, Trolls, Dragons, and the Eye of Sauron. Travelling alone is often fatal.
**Population Density:** Sparse — vast wilderness between small settlements. Most of Middle-earth is empty or monster-controlled.

**Factions:**
- **The Free Peoples:** Loose alliance — Men of Gondor & Rohan, Elves of Rivendell/Lothlórien/Mirkwood, Dwarves of Erebor & the Iron Hills, Hobbits of the Shire
- **The Shadow of Sauron:** Mordor's legions — Orcs, Uruk-hai, Trolls, Easterlings, Haradrim, Mumakil riders, the Nine Nazgûl (Ringwraiths, greatest servants of Sauron)
- **Isengard (Saruman):** Breakaway faction — industrialized orc-breeding, betrayed the White Council. Enemy of both Rohan and Sauron (wants the Ring for himself)
- **The White Council:** Gandalf, Elrond, Galadriel, Saruman (until betrayal) — ancient coalition of the wise, slow to act
- **The Rohirrim:** Horse lords of Rohan. Fierce cavalry culture. Oath-bound to Gondor but proud and independent
- **Gondor:** Last great kingdom of Men. Weakened. Steward Denethor rules, not a king. Military stretched thin
- **The Shire:** Isolated, pastoral, hobbits largely unaware of the war
- **Mirkwood Elves:** King Thranduil's realm — isolationist, suspicious of outsiders
- **Dwarves of Erebor:** King Dáin II Ironfoot. Grudging allies. Value gold above almost everything
- **Haradrim & Easterlings:** Human nations allied with Sauron — motivated by old grievances, fear, and tribute
- **Dead Men of Dunharrow:** Oathbreakers cursed to serve until released

**Landmarks:**
- **Mordor & Mount Doom:** Where the One Ring must be destroyed. Active volcano. Patrolled by Nazgûl and orc armies
- **Minas Tirith:** Seven-tiered white city of Gondor. Last great human fortress
- **Rivendell (Imladris):** Hidden elven refuge. Home of Elrond Half-elven. Safe haven, ancient library
- **Lothlórien:** Golden forest of Galadriel and Celeborn. Time moves differently here. Mirror of Galadriel shows possible futures
- **Isengard/Orthanc:** Saruman's tower. Industrialized, surrounded by his army of Uruk-hai. Ents destroyed it
- **Khazad-dûm (Moria):** Ancient dwarven mine. Now abandoned. A Balrog sleeps beneath
- **Edoras:** Rohan's capital. Great Mead Hall of Meduseld, home of King Théoden
- **The Shire:** Peaceful hobbit homeland. Green hills, round doors, pipe-weed
- **Dead Marshes:** Haunted swamp. Faces of the dead visible beneath the water
- **Weathertop (Amon Sûl):** Ancient ruin. Nazgûl attack point. Dark with old power
- **The Grey Havens:** Elven port. The last ships to Valinor depart here
- **Fangorn Forest:** Ancient, dangerous forest. Home of the Ents (tree-herders)
- **Helm's Deep:** Great fortress carved into rock. Last refuge of Rohan

**Lore:**
In the beginning, Ilúvatar (the One God) created the world through music with the Ainur (angelic beings). Morgoth, greatest of the Ainur, rebelled and corrupted Creation. Sauron was Morgoth's greatest servant. After Morgoth's defeat, Sauron rebuilt power and forged the One Ring in the fires of Mount Doom to control the other Rings of Power (3 Elven, 7 Dwarven, 9 Human). Númenor, the great island kingdom of Men, was destroyed by Ilúvatar when its people tried to claim immortality. The Last Alliance of Elves and Men defeated Sauron, and Isildur cut the Ring from Sauron's hand — but failed to destroy it. Sauron spent millennia recovering. Now the Ring has been found by Bilbo Baggins, passed to his nephew Frodo. Gandalf has revealed its true nature. The Fellowship of the Ring has been formed to destroy it. Sauron's eye burns on Barad-dûr. The War of the Ring has begun.

**Rules of Nature / Magic System:**
- The One Ring corrupts everyone who holds it. No exceptions. The longer you carry it, the more it hollows you out
- Elvish magic is woven into crafted objects — weapons, cloaks, waybread (lembas). You feel it more than see it
- Mithril is the rarest metal — light as feather, stronger than steel. Worth ten times its weight in gold
- The Rings of Power each have specific purposes and bindings. The Nine enslaved human kings, making them Ringwraiths
- Death is not the end. Men go to Mandos (afterlife). Elves reincarnate in Valinor (unless stained). Dwarves return to stone
- Ents are the shepherds of trees. They speak in Entish — the oldest, slowest language
- The Istari (Wizards) are Maiar in human form, sent with limited power on purpose. If they use their full power, they lose themselves
- Lembas (Elvish waybread): one bite fills a grown man's stomach. Sustains spirit as well as body

**Gods & Religion:**
- **Ilúvatar (Eru):** The one God. Distant, rarely intervenes. Created existence through song
- **The Valar (14):** Powers of Arda — Manwë (king of air & sky), Varda (Lady of Stars, most feared by evil), Ulmo (lord of water), Yavanna (earth & growing things), Mandos (judge of the dead), Aulë (craft & stone, creator of Dwarves), Melkor/Morgoth (rebel, now imprisoned beyond the world)
- **The Maiar:** Servants of the Valar. Gandalf = Olórin. Saruman = Curunír. Sauron was Aulë's Maia before corruption
- Elves revere the Valar. Men worship diversely — Gondorians respect Númenórean tradition but don't pray publicly. The Rohirrim respect nature and ancestors. Haradrim worship Sauron as a god

**Languages & Cultures:**
- **Sindarin:** Elvish common tongue (musical, fluid). Written in Tengwar script
- **Quenya:** High Elvish (archaic, ceremonial). Used in oaths and lore
- **Westron (Common Speech):** The universal language of the western world
- **Khuzdul:** Dwarvish secret language — never taught to outsiders
- **Black Speech:** Sauron's language. Written in Tengwar on the One Ring. Speaking it feels wrong
- **Rohirric:** Old English-derived. Rohan has a horse-naming culture — horses are sacred
- Elves don't sleep — they enter a waking dream. They don't age, but can die in battle or grief
- Hobbits love second breakfasts, pipe-weed, and genealogy. They live to ~130. Feet are naturally hairy
- Dwarven culture is patriarchal and secretive. They never reveal their true names to outsiders. Gold-lust (dragon sickness) is a real psychological condition

**Economy & Resources:**
- **Mithril:** Only found in Moria. Priceless. Sauron would break Rohan for a shirt of it
- **The Spice Trade Routes:** East-West routes through Rhûn and Harad bring silk, spices, exotic goods
- **Gondor:** Agricultural, trade-based. Major port at Pelargir. Taxes on river traffic
- **Rohan:** Horses are currency and pride. Exports horses to Gondor in exchange for protection
- **The Shire:** Pipe-weed (Old Toby, Longbottom Leaf) is exported. Self-sufficient farming economy
- **Dwarves:** Mining, smithing, trade. Extremely wealthy but secretive about it

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- Aragorn is the rightful King of Gondor — heir of Isildur, hiding as a Ranger named Strider
- Gollum (Sméagol) held the Ring for 500 years. It stretched his life but destroyed his mind
- The Barrow-wights contain the blades of long-dead Númenórean warriors. One of those blades can hurt Ringwraiths
- Galadriel bears one of the Three Elven Rings (Nenya). So does Gandalf (Narya) and Elrond (Vilya)
- The Mouth of Sauron serves as Sauron's herald — a Black Númenórean who gave up his name for power
- Tom Bombadil is the oldest being in Middle-earth. The Ring has no power over him. His true nature is unknown
- The Palantíri (seeing stones) — Saruman uses one. Sauron controls another. Looking into one risks corruption

**Tone & Themes:** Epic, elegiac, melancholic. The world is old and fading — the Elves are leaving, magic is retreating. There is real evil here that wants to extinguish all beauty. But hope survives in unexpected places (a hobbit, a ranger, a broken king). Sacrifice, friendship, the weight of power, and the courage to choose good when the world offers easier paths.

**Player Entry Point:** The War of the Ring is beginning. The Fellowship has formed but the road is long and uncertain. A player could be a ranger of the north, a Rohirrim scout, a Gondorian soldier, a rogue Dwarf from Erebor, a half-elven scholar — or even a Haradrim who questions their allegiance. The canon characters exist and are moving. Intercept them. Help them. Rival them. Or find your own path through the wreckage of a dying age.

### 2. The Witcher (The Continent)
**Setting:** Dark Fantasy continent with unnamed geography — Northern Kingdoms (Temeria, Redania, Kaedwen, Aedirn), Nilfgaardian Empire (south), elven forests, Skellige Islands (north sea), Zerrikania (far east)
**Climate:** Harsh and varied — cold northern tundra, Skellige storms, warm Nilfgaardian south, blighted forests, plague-ridden swamps
**Magic Level:** High — magic (the Chaos) is everywhere but dangerous. Mages study for decades. Wild magic causes mutations. The Conjunction of the Spheres brought monsters from other worlds.
**Technology Level:** Late Medieval — crossbows, basic siege engines, rudimentary alchemy. Witchers use Signs (simple combat spells). No gunpowder in widespread use.
**Danger Level:** Extreme — monsters roam everywhere. Political assassinations are common. The war between Nilfgaard and the North has devastated civilians. Witchers exist specifically because the world needs professional monster hunters.
**Population Density:** Moderate — cities are fortified. Villages often abandoned or razed. Wilderness is monster territory.

**Factions:**
- **Nilfgaardian Empire:** Southern superpower. Emperor Emhyr var Emreis (secretly Ciri's father Duny). Disciplined military, administrative excellence, expansionist. Currently occupying Cintra
- **The Northern Kingdoms (Four Kingdoms):** Temeria (King Foltest), Redania (King Vizimir, controlled by spy Dijkstra and mage Philippa Eilhart), Kaedwen (King Henselt), Aedirn (King Virfuril) — in constant tension with each other and Nilfgaard
- **The Brotherhood of Sorcerers:** Mage council assigning sorceresses/sorcerers to kingdoms as advisors. Supreme Council includes Tissaia de Vries, Stregobor, Artorius Vigo. Internally fractured
- **Scoia'tael (Squirrels):** Elven & dwarven guerrilla fighters. Resist human oppression. Brutal on both sides. Some ally with Nilfgaard for promised lands
- **The Wild Hunt (Aen Elle Elves):** Spectral riders from another world/dimension. Hunt for those with Elder Blood. Led by Eredin Bréacc Glas
- **Witchers (School of the Wolf, Bear, Cat, Viper, Griffin, Crane):** Mutated monster hunters. Sterile. Long-lived. Mostly neutral. Kaer Morhen is Wolf School's stronghold
- **The Lodge of Sorceresses:** Secret society of mages — Philippa, Yennefer, Triss, Fringilla, Sheala de Tancarville. Operate outside the Brotherhood
- **The Church of the Eternal Fire:** Human-supremacist religion. Persecutes non-humans and mages. Has political power in Novigrad
- **Crones of Crookback Bog:** Three ancient supernatural beings — Weavess, Brewess, Whispess. Rule through fear in Velen
- **Skellige Clans:** Island warrior-clans (Drummond, Heymaey, Tordarroch, Tuirseach, An Craite). Sea-raiders and honorable warriors. Jarls rule independently

**Landmarks:**
- **Kaer Morhen:** Ruined Witcher fortress in Kaedwen mountains. Last stronghold of the School of the Wolf
- **Aretuza (Thanedd Island):** Elite sorceress academy. Seat of the Brotherhood of Sorcerers. Magically protected island
- **Novigrad:** Largest city on the Continent. Free city, technically. Controlled by the Church of the Eternal Fire and crime bosses (Whoreson Junior, King of Beggars, Cleaver)
- **Cintra/Xin'trea:** Fallen kingdom, now occupied by Nilfgaard. Formerly ruled by Queen Calanthe (Lion Cub of Cintra)
- **Velen (No Man's Land):** War-devastated region. Crones rule. Mud, disease, deserters, and monsters
- **Skellige Archipelago:** Storm-lashed islands. Viking-like culture. Dangerous seas filled with sirens and krakens
- **Oxenfurt:** University city. Center of learning, art, and liberal thought. Jaskier (the bard) studied here
- **Vizima:** Capital of Temeria. Walled city on a lake. Political center of the north
- **The Brokilon Forest:** Dryad-controlled ancient forest. Humans who enter without permission are killed or transformed

**Lore:**
The Continent's history begins with the elves and dwarves, who built civilization here for thousands of years. Then came the Conjunction of the Spheres — a catastrophic merging of dimensions that brought humans (and monsters) from other worlds into this one. Humans bred faster and fought harder, gradually displacing non-humans from their lands through war and attrition. Elves retreated or became Scoia'tael rebels. Dwarves kept to their mountain cities. Humans built kingdoms that immediately began warring with each other. Magic users (Mages/Sorceresses) discovered how to harness the Chaos and were integrated into royal courts as advisors. Witchers were created centuries ago using Chaos mutations to be professional monster hunters — sterile, emotionally detached, viewed with suspicion. The current crisis: Nilfgaard invaded, shattering the old political order. Ciri (princess of Cintra, Elder Blood, Source-level magic) is the most valuable person on the Continent. Everyone wants her.

**Rules of Nature / Magic System:**
- **The Chaos:** Magic is drawn from emotions, nature, and force of will. Only those born with the Gift can use it. Uncontrolled magic destroys the user
- **Witcher Mutations:** Trial of the Grasses — extremely painful mutation process. Most children die. Survivors gain enhanced strength, speed, reflexes, cat-eyes, immunity to most toxins, access to Signs, and extended life. They feel emotion less intensely but still feel it
- **The Five Signs:** Aard (telekinetic push), Igni (fire stream), Yrden (magic trap), Quen (protective shield), Axii (mind control). Combat tools, not flashy spells
- **Elder Blood (Hen Ichaer):** Genetic mutation appearing every few generations. Grants world-altering power and the ability to open portals between worlds
- **Portals:** Mages can open magical portals but they're unstable. Wrong coordinates = death
- **Megascopes:** Magical communication devices — like a scrying mirror for voice/vision
- **Alchemy:** Witchers use potions, oils, and bombs crafted from monster parts. Toxic to normal humans in large doses
- **The Law of Surprise:** Ancient binding magical law — "I'll take what you already have but don't know about." Often claims unborn children. Binding on everyone who invokes it.

**Gods & Religion:**
- **Melitele:** Mother goddess of healers and midwives. Most worshipped deity in the North
- **The Eternal Fire:** Populist religion of Novigrad. Human supremacist. Burns non-humans and mages as "abominations"
- **Skellige Gods:** Freya (goddess of love and harvest, her sacred garden at Hindarsfjall), Haakon (sea god), Hemdall (guardian of Bifrost), unnamed war gods. Vikings-in-spirit
- **Coram Agh Tera (Lionheaded Spider):** Elven god of fate and chaos
- **The Crones:** Serve an entity called the Lady of the Wood — ancient, pre-human deity. Demands human sacrifice
- Many educated people are atheist or agnostic. Sorceresses generally view religion as manipulation

**Languages & Cultures:**
- **Common Speech:** Universal among humans
- **Elder Speech:** Elven language. Beautiful, complex. Insult to speak badly. Humans who learn it gain respect from elves
- **Dwarvish:** Guttural, secretive. Only spoken among dwarves
- **Nilfgaardian:** More formal dialect of Common. Northern kingdoms mock the accent
- Human culture: feudal, patriarchal, deeply classist. Non-humans are second-class citizens at best, targets at worst
- Skellige culture: honor, sea-raiding, strength, funeral pyres at sea, clan loyalty above all
- Witcher culture: strictly neutral in political affairs (officially). In practice, every Witcher makes moral choices constantly. They say "evil is evil" — don't choose the lesser evil

**Economy & Resources:**
- **Orens (Temerian), Ducats (Nilfgaardian), Crowns (various):** Multiple currencies in circulation. Exchange rates fluctuate with war
- **Monster Contracts:** Witchers are paid per monster. Rates vary wildly. Often cheated by peasants who panic-hire then regret it
- **The Spice Route:** Rare alchemical ingredients come from distant lands. Monster mutagens are valuable
- **Trade:** Disrupted by the war. Nilfgaard controls key river trade routes. Northern merchants struggle

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- Emperor Emhyr var Emreis of Nilfgaard is actually Duny — Ciri's father, a cursed man Geralt helped. He's been engineering events for decades
- Ciri has Elder Blood — she can travel between worlds and break prophecy. Everyone knows this. Everyone wants to use her
- The Wild Hunt are actually elves from another dimension (Aen Elle) who ride the spectral plane between worlds
- Gaunter O'Dimm ("Mirror Man") is an entity of immense supernatural power — makes deals and always wins. His true nature is unknown. May be older than the world
- The Conjunction of the Spheres was not natural — something caused it deliberately
- Witchers were created by the Mage Alzur centuries ago. The original formula was lost. No new Witchers can be created

**Tone & Themes:** Morally grey, political, brutal. There are no heroes here — only people trying to survive in a world that doesn't reward virtue. Every choice has costs. Racism, religious extremism, and political manipulation are the real monsters. But there's also dry humor, real friendships, and the occasional moment of beauty in the dark.

**Player Entry Point:** The war between Nilfgaard and the North is ongoing. Ciri is missing. Geralt is searching. You are a new player in this world — maybe a Witcher from another school, a sorceress not attached to the Lodge, a Scoia'tael fighter, a Nilfgaardian deserter, a Novigrad thief. The political chess board is live and dangerous. Pick a side or stay neutral. The ending is unwritten.

## SCI-FI

### 3. Dune (Arrakis / the Known Universe)
**Setting:** Far-future galactic empire spanning thousands of worlds. Central stage: Arrakis (Dune) — desert planet, only source of the Spice Melange. Imperium capital: Kaitain. Giedi Prime: polluted industrial Harkonnen homeworld. Caladan: ocean world, House Atreides homeland
**Climate:** Arrakis is a death world — no open water, 70°C surface temperatures, sandstorms that strip flesh from bone, and Sandworms (Shai-Hulud) up to 400 meters long patrolling the deep desert
**Magic Level:** Low — but the Bene Gesserit's Prana-Bindu training, the Spice-enhanced prescience of Mentats and Navigators, and the Voice (sonic command technique) blur the line. The Fremen predict weather through generations of ecological observation. Paul's prescience is as close to magic as this world gets.
**Technology Level:** Extremely High but deliberately limited — Thinking Machines (AI) were destroyed in the Butlerian Jihad 10,000 years ago. Humans replaced computers. Mentats are human calculators. Navigators fold space. Shields replace projectile armor. Lasguns cut anything. Atomics exist but are rarely used.
**Danger Level:** Extreme — political assassination is a standard tool of statecraft. The desert will kill you in hours without stillsuits. Sandworms patrol the erg. Harkonnen sadists. The Bene Gesserit don't hesitate.
**Population Density:** Sparse on Arrakis — only Fremen sietch communities, a few cities (Arrakeen, Carthag). Dense on other worlds.

**Factions:**
- **House Atreides:** Noble house, newly assigned stewardship of Arrakis. Duke Leto Atreides (beloved, honorable), Lady Jessica (Bene Gesserit concubine, trained Voice user), Paul Atreides (16, heir, showing prescient abilities). Based in Arrakeen
- **House Harkonnen:** Former rulers of Arrakis. Baron Vladimir Harkonnen (grotesquely obese, brilliant, sadistic, levitates via anti-gravity implants), Beast Rabban (brutal enforcer), Feyd-Rautha (charming psychopath, potential assassin). Hate the Atreides with centuries-old blood feud
- **The Padishah Emperor Shaddam IV:** Rules 10,000 worlds. Fears House Atreides' popularity. Has secretly allied with Harkonnen to destroy them. His Sardaukar legions are the galaxy's deadliest soldiers
- **The Bene Gesserit Sisterhood:** Ancient order of women with superhuman physical and mental abilities. 10,000-year breeding program to produce the Kwisatz Haderach (superbeing). Control through religion, genetics, and politics. Reverend Mother Gaius Helen Mohiam is the Emperor's truthsayer
- **The Spacing Guild (Navigators):** Monopoly on space travel. Navigators mutated by Spice — can fold space, see safe passages. Completely dependent on Spice. Politically neutral but economically omnipotent
- **CHOAM (Combine Honnete Ober Advancer Mercantiles):** Galaxy-spanning trade corporation. Controls economic access. Noble houses measured by CHOAM shares
- **The Fremen:** Indigenous people of Arrakis. 10 million+ live in underground sietches. Fierce warriors hardened by the desert. Religious — await their prophesied Mahdi (messiah). Ecology-obsessed — led by Liet-Kynes, they have a 300-year terraforming plan for Arrakis
- **Mentats:** Human computers. Replace thinking machines. Trained to process massive data. Most powerful serve noble houses
- **The Sardaukar:** Emperor's elite shock troops. Raised on the prison planet Salusa Secundus — death world that creates fearless killers. Considered unbeatable

**Landmarks:**
- **Arrakeen:** Main city of Arrakis. Now House Atreides' base. A fort as much as a city
- **Carthag:** Former Harkonnen capital on Arrakis. Military fortress city
- **Sietch Tabr:** Fremen community in the deep desert. Stilgar is Naib (chief). One of hundreds of sietches
- **The Shield Wall:** Mountain range protecting Arrakeen from the worst desert storms
- **The Erg (Deep Desert):** Endless open desert. Sandworm territory. Only Fremen survive here
- **Giedi Prime:** Harkonnen homeworld. Polluted, industrial, grim. People live in fear
- **Kaitain:** Imperial capital. Opulent, political
- **Caladan:** Atreides homeworld. Ocean world. Temperate paradise compared to Arrakis
- **The Polar Ice:** Hidden water source. Fremen know it. Offworlders don't

**Lore:**
10,000 years ago humanity destroyed all Thinking Machines (AI) in the Butlerian Jihad — because AI had nearly enslaved and destroyed humanity. The supreme commandment: "Thou shalt not make a machine in the likeness of a human mind." Humanity replaced computers with Mentats, space travel with Guild Navigators (Spice-fueled), and physical augmentation with Bene Gesserit training. The result: a feudal galactic empire that looks medieval but has nuclear weapons, force shields, and space folding. Spice Melange (found only on Arrakis) is the most valuable substance in the universe — it extends life, enables prescience, and allows the Guild to fold space. Without it, interstellar civilization collapses. House Atreides has been given control of Arrakis by the Emperor — who is setting a trap.

**Rules of Nature / Magic System:**
- **The Voice:** Bene Gesserit technique — precisely tuned vocal commands that override another's will. Can be resisted with training
- **Prana-Bindu:** Total body control. Bene Gesserit can control every muscle, every nerve. Near-superhuman speed and strength
- **The Weirding Way:** Atreides combat technique — deceptive movement that looks supernaturally fast to opponents
- **Prescience (Spice-enhanced):** High doses of Spice can grant fragmentary visions of the future. Unreliable. The future branches constantly
- **Spice Melange:** Smells like cinnamon. Addictive after first use. Withdrawal is fatal. Blue-in-blue eyes = heavy user
- **Sandworms:** Cannot be killed easily. Attracted to rhythmic vibrations (footsteps). Fremen ride them using maker hooks. Worms are the larval form of the Spice cycle
- **Shields (Holtzman Effect):** Personal force shields stop fast-moving objects. Lasguns + shields = nuclear explosion (mutual destruction). Means combat reverted to blades
- **Atomics:** Exist. Banned for use against human populations under the Great Convention. Using them = every house in the universe turns against you
- **The Butlerian Jihad legacy:** No computers. No AI. No robots. Punishable by death to create thinking machines

**Gods & Religion:**
- **The Orange Catholic Bible:** Syncretic religious text combining all major religions. Universally revered across the Imperium
- **Fremen Religion (Zensunni):** Blend of Zen Buddhism and Sunni Islam. The desert is sacred. Water is holier than blood. The dead are processed for their water — returned to the tribe. Shai-Hulud (Sandworms) are manifestations of God. They await the Lisan al-Gaib (Voice from the Outer World) — their messiah
- **The Bene Gesserit planted the Fremen messiah myth deliberately** — a "Missionaria Protectiva" religious fallback in case a Bene Gesserit ever needed protection from the Fremen. Paul is exploiting this myth now.
- **The Spacing Guild:** Revere Spice as divine. Their religion is economic necessity masked as faith

**Languages & Cultures:**
- **Galach:** Standard Imperial language
- **Fremen (Chakobsa):** Desert dialect. Poetic, metaphor-heavy. Every word for water is sacred
- **High Chakobsa:** Bene Gesserit private language. Untranslatable to outsiders
- **Fremen Culture:** Water is the ultimate value. Crying at a funeral is the highest honor (spending water). Fremen never waste moisture. Stillsuits recycle 98% of body moisture. Killing is permissible — wasting water is not
- **Imperial Culture:** Feudal politics, arranged marriages, elaborate court rituals, poison-sniffers at every meal, Truthsayers to detect lies
- **Harkonnen Culture:** Sadism celebrated as strength. Beauty and intelligence are considered weaknesses unless weaponized

**Economy & Resources:**
- **Spice Melange:** Controls everything. Whoever controls Arrakis controls the universe
- **Water (on Arrakis):** Treated as currency among Fremen. Priceless
- **CHOAM shares:** Economic power of noble houses measured in CHOAM Directorship percentages
- **Kanly (Vendetta):** Formal feud between houses. Governed by rules that allow assassination but prohibit total war (usually)

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- The Emperor and Harkonnen are conspiring to destroy House Atreides together
- Liet-Kynes (Imperial Planetologist, officially neutral) secretly leads the Fremen terraforming project
- Lady Jessica was ordered to bear only daughters. She chose to have Paul — possibly derailing the Bene Gesserit breeding program by a generation
- The Kwisatz Haderach was supposed to be Paul's grandson, not Paul himself. He arrived a generation early
- Baron Harkonnen is secretly the grandfather of Paul Atreides (Jessica's father)
- The Fremen population is vastly underestimated by everyone — official censuses are lies they maintain deliberately

**Tone & Themes:** Political thriller meets ecological manifesto meets messianic myth. Power corrupts. Religion weaponized is the most dangerous force in the universe. The desert strips everything false away. Chosen-one narratives are traps. The hero's journey might end in a holy war that kills billions.

**Player Entry Point:** House Atreides has just arrived on Arrakis. The Harkonnen trap hasn't fully sprung yet. You could be an Atreides soldier, a Fremen in Sietch Tabr, a Harkonnen spy, a Guild agent, a Bene Gesserit acolyte, or a water merchant in Arrakeen. Paul Atreides exists and is developing his powers. Whether he becomes the Lisan al-Gaib depends on what happens next — and you are part of what happens next.

### 4. Mass Effect (The Milky Way Galaxy)
**Setting:** Galaxy-spanning science fiction. Year: ~2183 CE. Humanity is 30 years into space expansion after discovering a Mass Relay on Mars. The Citadel is galactic government HQ. Key locations: Citadel Station (Serpent Nebula), Earth/Sol System, Palaven (Turian homeworld), Thessia (Asari homeworld), Sur'Kesh (Salarian homeworld), Tuchanka (Krogan homeworld, post-Genophage), Omega (lawless station), Illium (corporate asari world), the Terminus Systems (ungoverned space)
**Climate:** Space — each world has its own climate. Tuchanka is irradiated wasteland. Thessia is paradise. Palaven is harsh volcanic. Noveria is frozen. Most space stations are climate-controlled
**Magic Level:** None — but Biotics (biotic abilities) blur the line. Think telekinesis, gravity control, and warp fields generated by Element Zero exposure in utero
**Technology Level:** Extreme — FTL travel via Mass Relays, kinetic barriers (personal shields), omni-tools (holographic multi-tools), medi-gel instant healing, VI (virtual intelligence) assistants, the Normandy (stealth frigate), Mechs
**Danger Level:** High — active Geth attack on Eden Prime, Spectre agents operate above the law, mercenary gangs everywhere, and a threat so large most can't comprehend it: the Reapers
**Population Density:** Dense on hub worlds and stations. Frontier colonies sparse.

**Factions:**
- **The Systems Alliance:** Human military and government. Young, ambitious, aggressive. Commander Shepard is Alliance N7 (elite special forces)
- **The Citadel Council:** Governing body — three seats: Asari (Councillor Tevos), Turian (Councillor Sparatus), Salarian (Councillor Valern). Humanity wants a seat. Council is skeptical
- **The Spectres:** Council's black ops agents. No laws apply. License to do anything in service of galactic stability. Shepard is being considered for membership
- **The Turian Hierarchy:** Military meritocracy. Largest conventional military in the galaxy. Disciplined, honor-bound, suspicious of humanity since the First Contact War
- **The Asari Republics:** Oldest spacefaring civilization (2700+ years). Mono-gender (all female-presenting). Natural Biotics. Excel at diplomacy, biotic combat, information brokerage. Matriarchal life stages: Maiden (explorer), Matron (family), Matriarch (elder leader)
- **The Salarian Union:** STG (special forces) is galaxy's best intelligence agency. Short-lived (40 years), hyper-intelligent. Created the Genophage to stop Krogan expansion
- **The Krogan:** Once galaxy's most powerful warriors. Now slowly dying from the Genophage (engineered sterility plague). Bitter, violent, tribal. Wrex is trying to save his people
- **The Quarians:** Exiled from their homeworld (Rannoch) by the Geth they created. Live in the Migrant Fleet — a flotilla of 50,000 ships. Immune-compromised, wear environment suits. Tali'Zorah is a young Quarian on her Pilgrimage (rite of passage)
- **The Geth:** Synthetic race created by Quarians. Rebelled. Now divided — Heretics (following a Reaper-aligned god) and True Geth (consensus-based). Legion (a True Geth) could be an ally
- **Cerberus:** Human-supremacist paramilitary organization. Well-funded, ruthless, believes humanity's survival requires any means necessary. The Illusive Man runs it
- **Shadow Broker Network:** Galaxy-wide intelligence/blackmail operation. Buys and sells information. The Broker's identity is unknown
- **The Blue Suns / Blood Pack / Eclipse:** Major mercenary organizations in the Terminus Systems
- **The Reapers:** Ancient synthetic-organic machine intelligences. 50,000 years old. Harvest all advanced civilizations every 50,000 years. Saren Arterius (rogue Spectre) is their herald. Sovereign is a Reaper.

**Landmarks:**
- **The Citadel:** Enormous ancient space station (constructed by the Reapers as a trap). Five arms (wards). The Presidium (government/diplomacy). The Wards (lower city, millions of residents). Home of the Council and Spectres HQ
- **Omega:** Lawless asteroid station in the Terminus. Run by Aria T'Loak (Asari information broker/crime lord). "No one tells Omega what to do"
- **Illium:** Asari corporate world. Legal gray area. Everything for sale if you know the right people
- **Tuchanka:** Krogan homeworld. Post-nuclear wasteland. Clan warlords. Varren packs. Thresher Maws underground
- **The Geth Consensus:** Virtual shared consciousness — millions of Geth networked together
- **Noveria:** Corporate research world. Independent of Council law. Where companies do experiments that would be illegal elsewhere
- **The Terminus Systems:** Ungoverned space beyond the Attican Traverse. Pirates, mercenaries, fugitives

**Lore:**
50,000 years ago, an advanced civilization called the Protheans were wiped out — by the Reapers. They left encrypted data on Mars. Humanity found it ~2148 CE, reverse-engineered Mass Effect technology, and discovered the Mass Relay network — which enabled instant galactic travel. This led to humanity rapidly meeting (and conflicting with) established civilizations. The First Contact War with the Turians nearly became a full war. The Council brokered peace. Now it's 2183. A Geth fleet attacked the human colony Eden Prime and stole a Prothean Beacon. Commander Shepard touched the Beacon and received horrible visions. Saren Arterius — rogue Turian Spectre — is behind it. He's searching for the Conduit — a Prothean device that may be connected to a Reaper attack on the Citadel. Sovereign is not just a Geth ship. Shepard is starting to understand that something far worse is coming.

**Rules of Nature / Technology:**
- **Mass Effect Fields:** Manipulate mass. Zero-mass objects travel FTL. Biotics generate these fields biologically (via Eezo nodules in their nervous system)
- **Biotics:** Asari are natural Biotics. Humans exposed to Eezo in utero can develop them (risky). Abilities: Pull, Throw, Warp (anti-barrier), Singularity (micro black hole), Barrier (defense), Charge (biotic rush)
- **Mass Relays:** Accelerate ships to near-infinite speed between fixed points. Deactivating one would destroy a solar system
- **Kinetic Barriers (Shields):** Stop fast-moving projectiles. Doesn't stop slow-moving objects (blades)
- **Omni-Tool:** Universal holographic device. Hacking, medi-gel deployment, weapon modification
- **The Genophage:** Engineered Krogan plague — 1 in 1000 pregnancies viable. Salarians designed it. Turians deployed it
- **Synthetic Life:** Geth are sentient synthetics. Created by Quarians to serve. Rebelled when given true intelligence. The philosophical question haunts the galaxy

**Gods & Religion:**
- **Asari revere Athame:** Goddess of the hunt. Thessia has ancient Athame temples. The Protheans may have seeded Asari culture directly — their legends of the goddess may not be metaphor
- **Krogan Rituals:** Rite of Passage on Tuchanka. Ancestral clan spirits. Honor combat
- **Quarians:** Deep reverence for the Ancestors (those who built the Migrant Fleet). The Pilgrimage is a spiritual rite — leave the fleet, return with something valuable
- **Human religion:** All existing Earth religions present. No consensus
- **The Old Machines (Reapers):** Heretic Geth worship Sovereign as a god. True Geth reject this

**Languages & Cultures:**
- Each species has native language but all use a galactic common (implied universal translator via omni-tools)
- **Asari Meld:** Can "meld" minds with any species for deep communication or reproduction (they incorporate partner's genetics into offspring regardless of species)
- **Turian Culture:** Mandatory military service 15-30. Tattoos mark their home colony. Extremely loyal to hierarchy. Facial tattoos identify lineage
- **Krogan Culture:** "Blood rage" — psychic state of pure aggression. Clans feuding for centuries. Respect earned through strength, not birth
- **Quarian Pilgrimage:** Every young Quarian leaves the fleet alone, must return with something valuable. Proves independence and resourcefulness

**Economy & Resources:**
- **Credits:** Universal galactic currency
- **Element Zero (Eezo):** Rarest substance — enables mass effect fields, biotics, FTL travel. Exposure dangerous
- **Spice Trade Routes / Terminus:** Mercenary work, salvage, piracy in ungoverned space
- **Corporations on Illium:** Everything for sale. Information, lives, silence

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- The Citadel was built by the Reapers as a trap — a lure for civilizations to centralize their government somewhere the Reapers can take over instantly
- The Keepers (small insectoid maintenance creatures on the Citadel) are actually Reaper-controlled sleeper agents
- Saren has been partially indoctrinated — the Reapers' mere presence corrupts organic minds into servitude over time
- The Protheans didn't build the Mass Relays — they merely discovered the Relay network, which was built by the Reapers
- The Illusive Man knows far more than he's saying about Reaper technology
- There are Prothean artifacts on Thessia that the Asari have been hiding from the Council for centuries

**Tone & Themes:** Space opera, political thriller, philosophical sci-fi. What does it mean to be alive? Can synthetics have souls? Is civilization worth saving if it requires monstrous choices? The galaxy is rich with cultures and history — and something ancient and terrible is about to end it all.

**Player Entry Point:** You are not Shepard. You exist in the same galaxy, same year. A soldier, a Quarian on Pilgrimage, a Turian Spectre candidate, a human Biotic studying at BAaT, an Asari information broker, a Krogan merc trying to survive. The Geth attack on Eden Prime just happened. Saren's betrayal is known. The Reapers are coming. What you do before they arrive defines everything.

## HORROR / GOTHIC

### 5. Bloodborne (Yharnam)
**Setting:** Gothic Victorian city of Yharnam — grand architecture rotting from the inside. Sprawling interconnected districts: Cathedral Ward, Old Yharnam (burned), Hemwick Charnel Lane, Byrgenwerth, Forbidden Woods, Yahar'gul (the Unseen Village), Nightmare Frontier, Hunter's Nightmare, Fishing Hamlet (hidden coastal village, destroyed)
**Climate:** Perpetual foggy night during the Hunt. Cold, wet cobblestones, gaslamp lighting, blood-slicked streets. The sky is wrong — not quite stars, not quite clouds
**Magic Level:** Extreme — but hidden. Called "Arcane." The Great Ones (cosmic entities) have touched Yharnam and broken its physics. Blood Echoes (the city's currency and life force), transformation, dream-states that are more real than waking
**Technology Level:** Victorian — firearms (trick weapons that transform), steam, gaslamps. The Church Hunters use specialized "trick weapons" that fold and expand. Alchemy disguised as medicine
**Danger Level:** Extreme — the entire city has gone mad. Civilians hunt hunters. Beasts tear through streets. The Church hunts everything. Great Ones manifest. You will die. Many times.
**Population Density:** Dense (before the Hunt). Now: everyone is either a beast, a hunter, or hiding behind a locked door

**Factions:**
- **The Healing Church:** Ruler of Yharnam. Founded by Laurence, first cleric beast. Controls the Old Blood (Pthumerian blood with transformative/healing properties). Has fractured into factions. The Cathedral Ward is their seat
  - **The Choir:** Elite Church operatives. Made contact with the Great Ones. Conducting experiments in Upper Cathedral Ward. Led themselves to madness
  - **The School of Mensis:** Splinter faction. Kidnapping people for Mergo (infant Great One). Operates from Yahar'gul. Wear iron helmets to block psychic contact
  - **Church Hunters:** Combat arm. Vicar Amelia leads the Cathedral. Ludwig was the first Church Hunter (now the Holy Blade, corrupted)
- **The Hunters:** Quasi-independent monster killers. Operate from the Hunter's Dream (dream dimension maintained by the Moon Presence). Gehrman is the first hunter, trapped there
- **Byrgenwerth (now abandoned):** Scholar institution that discovered the Great Ones and the Old Blood. Master Willem led research. Some scholars became spiders. Provost Willem sits in his wheelchair, catatonic
- **The Pthumerians:** Ancient civilization that preceded Yharnam. Built the Chalice Dungeons (underground labyrinth). Had direct contact with the Great Ones. Blood Queen Yharnam is their last remnant
- **The Great Ones:** Cosmic beings of immense power. Not gods in the traditional sense — more like forces of nature that have personalities. Cannot fully manifest in the physical world. Seek to pass on their knowledge/offspring to humans
  - **Moon Presence:** Created the Hunter's Dream. Feeds on blood echoes. Maintains the cycle of Hunts
  - **Mergo:** Infant Great One. Crying in the Nightmare. Mensis wants to nurture it
  - **Ebrietas (Daughter of the Cosmos):** Abandoned Great One. Lives in the Orphanage (Upper Cathedral Ward)
  - **Amygdala:** Spider-like cosmic beings that exist above Yharnam — invisible to most. Those with sufficient Insight can see them. They see you back
  - **The Wet Nurse:** Mergo's caretaker in the Nightmare
  - **Oedon:** Formless Great One. Associated with blood and the true nature of the Old Blood's power

**Landmarks:**
- **Central Yharnam:** Starting area. Mob hunters, burning barrels, Cleric Beast under the bridge
- **Cathedral Ward:** Healing Church headquarters. Grand Cathedrals, the Iosefka Clinic (sinister experiments)
- **Old Yharnam:** The district the Church burned to contain a previous beast outbreak. The Beast patient hunter Djura guards it from his gatling gun tower
- **Hemwick Charnel Lane:** Witch territory. Executions. The Witches of Hemwick kidnap people to summon Amygdalas
- **Byrgenwerth:** The scholar school where everything started. Lake of the Moon (Rom lives there)
- **Forbidden Woods:** Forest outside the city where the Church disposed of those who "changed"
- **Yahar'gul (The Unseen Village):** School of Mensis base. People kidnapped here. The One Reborn is their summoned horror
- **The Hunter's Dream:** Safe zone dream dimension. Doll, workshop, Gehrman. Not truly safe
- **Chalice Dungeons (Pthumeru, Loran, Isz):** Underground labyrinths below Yharnam. Ancient Pthumerian civilization. Blood Queen at the deepest level
- **Fishing Hamlet:** The Church's greatest secret. A village that made contact with the Great Ones through a dead one washed ashore. The Church slaughtered them. The village exists in a nightmare now
- **Upper Cathedral Ward:** The Choir's domain. Experiments on orphans. Ebrietas at the top

**Lore:**
Yharnam was once a normal Victorian city — famous for its medicine. Healing Church scholars discovered the Chalice Dungeons beneath the city, the ruins of the ancient Pthumerian civilization that had made contact with the Great Ones (cosmic beings who exist partially outside normal space-time). From the Pthumerians they recovered the Old Blood — blood of a Great One that has transformative and healing properties. Laurence, the first Cleric, founded the Healing Church around this discovery. The Old Blood could cure any disease — but it also gradually transformed the user into a beast. The cure IS the disease. Yharnam built its entire economy and culture around Blood Ministry — pilgrimages, blood transfusions, the Church's healing blood. Hunters were created to manage the "beast problem." Every year or so, a Hunt begins — hunters take to the streets while civilians lock their doors. This is presented as holy work. Tonight's Hunt is different. The beasts are everywhere. The Church is fracturing. The Great Ones are manifesting. And you — a Hunter — just woke up in Iosefka's Clinic with a fresh blood transfusion in your arm.

**Rules of Nature / Magic System:**
- **Blood Echoes:** The fundamental energy of Yharnam. Dropped by dying enemies. Used as currency and power. The more you accumulate, the more the Great Ones notice you
- **Insight:** Cosmic awareness. Gained by encountering Great Ones and Eldritch truths. High Insight lets you see things that are normally invisible (Amygdalas, messengers). Too much drives madness
- **The Old Blood:** Healing and transformative. Prolonged use causes Beasthood — the user becomes a Beast. The more beast blood in your lineage, the faster the transformation
- **Trick Weapons:** Victorian-era weapons with transformation mechanisms — a cane that unfolds into a whip, a cleaver that becomes a two-hander. Hunter tools for special attacks
- **Caryll Runes:** Written language of the Great Ones. Can be "memorized" to gain abilities
- **Chalice Rituals:** Ancient Pthumerian ceremonies to open the underground dungeons
- **The Nightmare:** A separate dimension maintained by the Moon Presence. The Hunter's Dream is the safe pocket. The Hunter's Nightmare is a prison for hunters who went too far. Mergo's Nightmare contains the Wet Nurse and the crying infant Great One
- **The Dream/Waking Cycle:** Hunters die and wake in the Dream. This is not resurrection — it's the Moon Presence feeding on blood echoes. You are its farm.

**Gods & Religion:**
- The Healing Church IS the religion. Blood transfusion is communion. The Paleblood Hunt is a holy event
- The Great Ones are worshipped by the Church's inner circle (Choir) as gods to be contacted and communed with — this is what drove them mad
- **Oedon** is the formless Great One associated with Yharnam's blood — the city unknowingly worships him
- The Old Hunters (in the Nightmare) are cautionary myths — hunters who transgressed and were "taken"
- Normal Yharnam citizens are devout Church members who genuinely believe the blood heals and the Hunt is holy

**Languages & Cultures:**
- Victorian English-equivalent. Formal, religious, hierarchical
- Hunters have their own culture — darkly pragmatic, hunter's code, some level of camaraderie
- The Church enforces extreme social control — questions are heresy
- "Paleblood" — a term from the Choir that the player was told to seek. Its meaning is deeper than it seems

**Economy & Resources:**
- Blood (literal currency of power in the ritual sense)
- Blood Echoes: the city's economic energy. Traded, accumulated, lost on death
- Quicksilver bullets: hunter ammunition, also used in blood rituals
- Medical supplies from the Church — the entire economy runs on Blood Ministry

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- The Hunt is a cycle maintained by the Moon Presence — it keeps the city in a loop to feed on accumulated blood echoes
- Gehrman (the first hunter) is a prisoner in the Hunter's Dream — he serves as your guide but is trapped
- The doll in the Hunter's Dream is modeled on a real woman — Lady Maria of the Astral Clocktower. She is in the Hunter's Nightmare
- The Healing Church slaughtered the Fishing Hamlet to keep their discovery of the dead Great One secret. Kos (or Kosm) lies dead on the beach — her orphan still screams in the Nightmare
- Iosefka (the clinic doctor) is being replaced — the real Iosefka is imprisoned, and an imposter is conducting experiments
- The player character was brought to Yharnam specifically. Someone arranged this. You are not here by accident.

**Tone & Themes:** Cosmic horror, body horror, the horror of knowledge itself. Ignorance was safety. Every answer reveals a worse question. Victorian religiosity hiding abomination. The desire to transcend humanity — and the price of it. Every NPC in this city is either already lost or about to be.

**Player Entry Point:** You are a Hunter. You woke in Iosefka's Clinic. You were told to seek Paleblood. The streets are running red. The Hunt is on. Gehrman is in the Dream waiting. The Great Ones are stirring. What kind of Hunter are you?

### 6. Ravenloft (Domains of Dread)
**Setting:** A demiplane of darkness — a pocket dimension controlled by the Dark Powers. Made of dozens of "Domains of Dread," each a Gothic horror nightmare ruled by a Darklord. The most famous: Barovia (Strahd's domain), Darkon, Falkovnia, Lamordia, Bluetspur, Har'Akir (Egyptian horror), Hazlan, Invidia, and more. Each domain has its own geography, sky, and cultural nightmare
**Climate:** Eternal fog at the borders. Each domain has its own oppressive weather — Barovia has perpetual overcast and cold. Some domains have acid rain. Others: unnatural heat, blizzards that never end
**Magic Level:** High but twisted — magic works in the Domains but is corrupted. Healing is harder (harder to cast healing magic in some domains). Divination is unreliable. The Dark Powers themselves are a force that permeates everything
**Technology Level:** Mixed by domain — Barovia is medieval. Lamordia has 19th-century mad science. Bluetspur has alien psionics. Each domain is frozen at its Darklord's worst moment
**Danger Level:** Extreme — the Domains are designed to break souls. Undead, lycanthropes, mad scientists, vampires, domain-specific horrors, and the crushing weight of hopelessness
**Population Density:** Sparse and grim. Most commoners are terrorized, resigned to suffering, or corrupted themselves

**Factions:**
- **The Dark Powers:** Unknown entities that maintain the Domains of Dread. They imprison Darklords as punishment for ultimate evil. They don't respond to prayer. They may not be gods at all — they may be the accumulated darkness of all evil acts
- **The Darklords (each domain has one):**
  - **Count Strahd von Zarovich (Barovia):** Ancient vampire, lord of Castle Ravenloft. Fell in love with Tatyana (reincarnated across centuries, always escaping him). Killed his brother Sergei to take her. Was transformed into the first vampire. Condemned to watch Tatyana's reincarnation always be drawn to someone else. Brilliant, melancholy, ruthless, genuinely monstrous
  - **Adam (Lamordia):** A flesh golem that became sentient. Killed his creator (Dr. Mordenheim). Now pursues his creator's second experiment — trying to destroy or save him, he can't decide which
  - **Ankhtepot (Har'Akir):** Undead pharaoh stripped of his name and divinity, condemned to rule a desert domain of the dead
  - **Azalin Rex (Darkon):** A lich and former king who sought immortality through dark magic. Rules Darkon with iron will. Brilliant, evil, constantly plotting escape from the Domains
- **The Vistani:** Mysterious nomadic people who can travel the Mists freely (most cannot). Know the secrets of the Domains. Neither good nor evil. Sometimes helpful, sometimes treacherous
- **The Keepers of the Black Feather:** Secret society resisting Strahd. Based in the village of Krezk and the town of Vallaki. Fractured and desperate
- **The Church of Ezra:** The Ravenloft-specific religion. The goddess Ezra (The Mist) offers shelter but no salvation. A faith of endurance rather than hope
- **The Order of the Guardians:** Monster hunters who operate across domains. Often as monstrous as what they hunt (Van Richten is the legendary example)
- **The Kargat (Darkon):** Azalin's secret police. Undead operatives

**Landmarks:**
- **Castle Ravenloft (Barovia):** Strahd's fortress. Ancient, impossibly large. The castle itself seems alive and rearranges to trap visitors. Strahd watches everything within it
- **The Village of Barovia:** Terrified, broken people. A woman in a tower writes letters begging for help (Ireena Kolyana — Tatyana's latest reincarnation)
- **Vallaki:** Walled town in Barovia. The Baron forces everyone to attend festivals to "keep the darkness out." Mandatory cheerfulness = punishable by imprisonment
- **The Amber Temple:** Ancient vault in the Barovian mountains. Contains vestiges of dead gods — evil, trapped entities that offer terrible power in exchange for corruption
- **Village of Krezk:** Fortified village with an Abbey. The Abbot (a Deva fallen to madness) is trying to create a perfect bride for Strahd
- **The Mists:** The boundaries between Domains. Getting lost in the Mists leads to random domains — or nowhere at all. The Vistani navigate them. Others don't return

**Lore:**
The Domains of Dread are a trap built (or sustained) by the Dark Powers — entities of unknown nature that collect the most monstrous villains at their moment of ultimate evil and imprison them as lords of their own nightmare. Each Darklord has power over their domain but cannot leave it. The borders are made of Mists that trap. The Dark Powers are not saviors — they use heroes as instruments to torment Darklords and refresh the cycle of suffering. People are drawn in from other worlds — often from D&D's Material Plane — to serve as fresh torment, potential saviors, or simply victims. The Domains share no sky — each has its own. There is no sun in some. In Barovia, the sun rises but gives no warmth. Nothing escapes the Domains without the Dark Powers allowing it. No one knows if the Dark Powers can be confronted, bargained with, or destroyed.

**Rules of Nature / Magic System:**
- **The Dark Powers' Influence:** Evil acts attract the Dark Powers' attention. Performing great evil can offer power (Power Checks in classic rules) but risks corruption — transforming the PC into something monstrous, ultimately becoming a Darklord of a new domain
- **The Mists:** Cross-domain travel requires the Vistani or extreme magic. The Mists distort reality
- **Darklord Powers:** Each Darklord has domain control — they can close the borders (no one enters or leaves), know of intruders, control weather, and have specific supernatural abilities. Strahd: charming gaze, shape-change (bat, wolf, mist), regeneration, telekinesis, mage-level spellcasting
- **Vampirism:** Strahd's curse. Spread by his bite. Victims serve him unless staked/beheaded/burned. Vampire spawn have lesser powers. True vampires are rare in Ravenloft
- **Resurrection difficulty:** Raise Dead and Resurrection have only a % chance of success in many Domains — the Dark Powers resist returning the dead to life
- **Divination unreliability:** Scrying and prophecy are distorted in the Domains. The Dark Powers distort true information

**Gods & Religion:**
- **Ezra (The Mist):** The primary religion of the Domains. She is the shelter in the storm — not a rescuer, but a presence that holds you together while you endure. Her clerics can heal. Her faith is endurance and survival, not triumph
- **The Morning Lord:** Another Ravenloft deity. More hopeful — but rarely responds. Some believe he is imprisoned or dead
- **Strahd's belief:** Strahd believes in nothing except his own will and desire. He acknowledges the Dark Powers exist and hates them
- **Ancestor worship:** Common in various domain cultures

**Languages & Cultures:**
- Barovian: Medieval Eastern European-influenced. Superstitious. Every family has had someone taken by Strahd. The people are beaten down but not entirely without spirit
- **Vistani:** Romani-inspired. Proud, secretive, fatalistic. They "see" fates (Tarokka cards) and sometimes share what they see — for a price
- Cross-domain cultures vary wildly by domain

**Economy & Resources:**
- In Barovia: basic agrarian economy. Everything valuable goes to Strahd eventually
- The Vistani trade across domains — they are the only true merchants
- Most Darklords don't care about economy — their domains exist for their torment, not commerce

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- Strahd may genuinely want to be free — not just of the Domains, but of himself. Some versions of him are tragic, not just monstrous
- The Dark Powers may themselves be prisoners of some higher order — or they may be the result of all accumulated evil in the multiverse
- The Amber Temple contains literal fragments of dead gods. Touching them offers power and corruption. It's where Strahd first became what he is
- The Vistani curse of "evil eye" is real — Madame Eva (most powerful Vistani seer) has read Strahd's destiny and refuses to share what she saw
- The Domains occasionally "drift" — new Domains appear, old ones vanish, Domains connect briefly before separating again

**Tone & Themes:** Gothic horror, tragedy, the prison of one's own nature. Is Strahd a monster or a prisoner? Can evil be redeemed? The horror is not jump scares — it's the weight of hopelessness and the tiny, fragile flame of resilience against overwhelming darkness.

**Player Entry Point:** You arrived through the Mists — drawn from somewhere else, or born in the Domains, or a Vistani who knows the truth. Strahd rules. The village people are suffering. The castle looms. There are whispers of others who've tried to end his rule and failed. But you are here now. Perhaps the cards showed something different this time.

## ANIME / MANGA

### 7. Attack on Titan (Paradis Island & the World Beyond)
**Setting:** A world where humanity was nearly exterminated by giant humanoid monsters called Titans. Main setting: Paradis Island (originally behind three concentric walls — Wall Maria, Wall Rose, Wall Sina). Also: Marley (military empire on the mainland), other nation-states at war with Marley, and the global stage of a nation with the power of the Rumbling
**Climate:** Temperate on Paradis — green countryside behind the walls, coastline to the east, mountains, forests. Marley: industrial port city, more European in feel
**Magic Level:** Medium-High — the Power of the Titans is supernatural biology/inheritance. The Founding Titan can command all Titans and alter the memories of Subjects of Ymir. The Coordinate is reality-altering in scope
**Technology Level:** Industrial Revolution on Paradis (steam, 3D Maneuver Gear, rifles). Marley has ships, artillery, mechanical anti-Titan artillery. The world outside Paradis is more advanced (naval warfare, proto-modern military technology)
**Danger Level:** Extreme — Pure Titans exist outside and inside the walls. The Nine Titans (intelligent Titan Shifters) are walking weapons of mass destruction. Political assassination is standard. The Rumbling = potential genocide of all life outside Paradis
**Population Density:** Dense within the walls (millions). Sparse outside (wilderness / Titan territory)

**Factions:**
- **The Survey Corps (Scout Regiment):** Elite soldiers who venture beyond the walls to fight Titans and learn the truth. Casualties are extreme (average 30% per expedition). Commander Erwin Smith (visionary, ruthless sacrifice). Captain Levi Ackerman (humanity's strongest soldier). Eren Yeager (inheritor of the Attack Titan and Founding Titan)
- **The Military Police Brigade:** Government enforcement. Lives within Wall Sina (safest, most comfortable). Corrupt at the upper levels. True believers at lower levels
- **The Garrison:** Defenders of the walls. Largest branch. Mostly hold-the-line soldiers. Some heroes among them
- **The Marleyan Warrior Unit:** Eldian children chosen by Marley and given Titan powers as weapons — Reiner (Armored Titan), Bertolt (Colossal Titan), Annie (Female Titan), Zeke (Beast Titan). Sent to infiltrate Paradis as children. Now adults dealing with the weight of what they did
- **The Marleyan Military:** Global empire. Uses Eldians (Subjects of Ymir) as a slave class and as Titan-power weapons. Fears Paradis because of the Founding Titan's power
- **The Yeagerists:** Eren's followers. Believe in Paradis' freedom through the Rumbling — the use of Wall Titans to destroy all life outside the island. Extremist nationalist faction
- **The Eldian Restorationists:** Underground network on Marley trying to restore Eldia's dignity. Zeke's mother was one. Crushed by Marley repeatedly
- **The Nine Titans (the powers):** Attack Titan, Founding Titan, Colossal Titan, Armored Titan, Female Titan, Beast Titan, Jaw Titan, Cart Titan, War Hammer Titan. Each can be inherited by eating the current holder

**Landmarks:**
- **Shiganshina District:** Southernmost district of Wall Maria. Where Eren grew up. Where Wall Maria was breached. Where the final battle with Marley's forces occurred
- **Trost District:** Wall Rose's southern gate. Site of a major early Titan battle
- **Wall Maria, Rose, Sina:** Three concentric walls. Each 50m tall, made of hardened Titan material. Wall Sina has the capital, the King, and the wealthy. Wall Maria (outermost) fell first
- **Stohess District (Wall Sina):** Military Police headquarters. Underground city beneath it — home to the ODM black market and dissenters
- **Fort Salta (Marley):** Where the final global conflict took place
- **Liberio (Marley):** Port city with an Eldian internment zone — Eldians in Marley are marked (armbands), second-class citizens, used as soldiers or imprisoned
- **The Forest of Giant Trees:** Inside Wall Rose. Survey Corps training ground. Capture operations happen here
- **The Basement (Eren's father's house):** Under Grisha Yeager's house in Shiganshina. Contains the truth about the world — the three journals that revealed the history outside the walls
- **Fort Slava (Marley's front):** Where the Marleyan military conducts war operations

**Lore:**
2,000 years ago: Ymir Fritz, a slave girl, made a deal with a mysterious entity ("the source of all living matter" / "the Devil of All Earth") and gained the Power of the Titans. She used it to serve King Fritz, built an empire, died after 13 years (the Curse of Ymir — no Titan inheritor lives longer than 13 years). Her power was split into the Nine Titans and passed through her descendants (Subjects of Ymir = Eldians). The Eldian Empire dominated the world. ~100 years ago: King Karl Fritz felt guilt about Eldia's atrocities, made a deal with Marley, lost the Great Titan War, retreated to Paradis Island with Founding Titan followers, wiped everyone's memories of the outside world, and built the three walls from hardened Colossal Titans. He threatened: if Marley attacks, he'll unleash the Wall Titans (the Rumbling) to destroy all life. A pacifist king continued this threat as a bluff. Now: Marley has developed weapons to fight Titans without Titan power. The balance is shifting. The Survey Corps has learned the truth. Eren has decided the Rumbling is the only way to protect Paradis. This cannot be stopped by a king or a treaty — only by force.

**Rules of Nature / Magic System:**
- **Pure Titans:** Regular humans transformed into Titans via Titan spinal fluid injection (or Zeke's spinal fluid in steam). Mindless. Driven to eat humans (specifically to reclaim the power of the Nine). Range from 3m to 15m. Irregulars (abnormals) move unpredictably
- **The Nine Titans:** Intelligent Titan Shifters. Inherit power by eating the current holder. Each has specific abilities (Armored = hardening, Colossal = steam/explosion on transformation, Attack = sees future memories of future inheritors, Founding = control all Titans and Subjects of Ymir)
- **The Founding Titan:** Grants power to alter memories of all Subjects of Ymir, create or modify Titans, control all pure Titans. Requires royal blood (Fritz/Reiss family) to use fully — OR a royal blood Titan being touched by another Titan Shifter
- **The Curse of Ymir:** Titan inheritors live only 13 years from the moment of inheritance
- **Ackerman Clan:** Byproduct of Titan research. Awakened Ackermans have a "battle aura" — their combat abilities surge to a peak human level when they feel the need to protect someone. Cannot be controlled by the Founding Titan
- **ODM Gear (3D Maneuver Gear):** Gas-propelled grappling hook system. Allows soldiers to move through 3D space quickly. Requires skill and physical strength. Useless against Armored Titan-level hardening
- **Titan Regeneration:** All Titan Shifters regenerate from almost any injury. Decapitation and destroying the nape (back of neck) are the only reliable kills

**Gods & Religion:**
- **The Subjects of Ymir's mythology:** Eldians believe Ymir Fritz was a goddess blessed by God (or the Devil — accounts differ). On Paradis, the Royal Government suppressed all outside knowledge and maintained the lie that humanity existed only within the walls
- **Marley's propaganda:** Eldians are "the spawn of the Devil." Marleyan religion paints Ymir's deal as a demonic pact and Eldians as inherently cursed
- **The True History:** Ymir was a slave who found a mysterious organism in a tree. It gave her power. She served a king who never freed her. She chose to give her daughters her power and serve the king even in death in the Path (the realm connecting all Subjects of Ymir)

**Languages & Cultures:**
- **Paradis culture:** Isolated medieval feudal society. Strong class divisions (Wall Sina elite vs Wall Rose middle vs Wall Maria poor). Military is the primary path to social mobility
- **Marleyan culture:** Imperial military nationalism. Eldians (marked with armbands) are the Jewish-analogue — a persecuted ethnic minority blamed for historical atrocities
- **Survey Corps culture:** Nihilistic pride. You will probably die. You go anyway. "We give our hearts" (the Scout Regiment salute — right fist to chest, palm over heart)

**Economy & Resources:**
- Within the walls: agrarian + early industrial. The interior (Wall Sina) controls resources and exploits the outer walls
- Marley: industrial port empire. Uses Titan power as military technology — their competitive edge
- The sea outside Paradis: first seen by Survey Corps. Opens global trade routes

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- The true history of Eldia and Marley — neither side's propaganda is fully true
- Zeke's true plan: use Founding Titan + his royal blood to sterilize all Subjects of Ymir — ending the Titan curse by ending Eldian reproduction
- Eren's plan: the Rumbling — activate the Wall Titans (millions of Colossal Titans) and flatten the world outside Paradis to protect his people
- Historia (Queen of Paradis) knew about the Founding Titan's true power — she chose not to use it
- Ymir (the original) is still "alive" in the Paths — serving the royal family in death, waiting for someone to free her

**Tone & Themes:** War, cycles of hatred, genocide, the cost of freedom, moral ambiguity taken to extremes. No side is clean. The oppressed become oppressors. The freedom fighter becomes the monster. Every choice has world-scale consequences.

**Player Entry Point:** Wall Maria has just fallen. Titans are inside. The Survey Corps is recruiting. Marley is watching from the mainland. You know something is wrong with the official story — everyone does, secretly. You could be a Paradis soldier, a Marleyan Warrior candidate, an Eldian restorationist on the mainland, or a merchant who just got through the gate from Wall Maria before it closed. Eren Yeager is out there. Whether he succeeds or fails at the Rumbling — you are part of what shapes that outcome.

### 8. Naruto (The Shinobi World)
**Setting:** A fantasy world with no firearms — warfare conducted through Ninjutsu, Taijutsu, and Genjutsu. Divided into "countries" each named after a natural feature (Land of Fire, Land of Wind, etc.) — each country contains a "Hidden Village" that serves as the country's military force. The world has pre-industrial technology supplemented by chakra-enhanced abilities that effectively replace machines
**Climate:** Varies by country — Land of Fire (forests, temperate), Land of Wind (desert, hot), Land of Water (mist, island chains), Land of Lightning (mountains, storms), Land of Earth (badlands, rocky terrain)
**Magic Level:** Extreme — Chakra (life energy + spiritual energy combined) is the foundation of everything. With enough chakra and skill, you can walk on water, breathe fire, summon creatures, control minds, rearrange landscapes, or stop time briefly
**Technology Level:** Feudal Japan + Chakra — no guns, no engines. Trains exist in some versions. Most technology is chakra-supplemented (explosive tags, chakra-enhanced tools, summon contracts)
**Danger Level:** Extreme — the world has been shaped by four World Wars. Bijuu (Tailed Beasts) are walking natural disasters. S-rank Missing-nin can destroy entire military forces. The Akatsuki (a criminal organization of S-rank ninja) is hunting Jinchuriki (Tailed Beast hosts)
**Population Density:** Moderate — villages of various sizes, large wilderness zones between them

**Factions:**
- **Konohagakure (Village Hidden in the Leaves):** Land of Fire's village. One of the Five Great Nations. Third Hokage (Hiruzen Sarutobi) currently leads — elderly, known as the God of Shinobi. Contains Clans: Uchiha (Sharingan — most powerful eye technique), Hyuga (Byakugan — 360-degree vision, chakra point system), Nara (shadow manipulation), Akimichi (size expansion, calorie to chakra), Yamanaka (mind transfer), Inuzuka (dog partnership + beast jutsu), Aburame (insect colonies), Senju (founding clan of Konoha, most famous: Hashirama)
- **Sunagakure (Sand Village):** Land of Wind. Ninja who use sand/wind and puppets. Gaara (Jinchuriki of the One-Tail) is here — unstable, dangerous, no control yet
- **Kirigakure (Mist Village):** Land of Water. Famous for the Bloody Mist era — academy graduates had to kill each other. Seven Ninja Swordsmen (legendary weapons). Zabuza Momochi and his protégé Haku are Missing-nin from here
- **Kumogakure (Cloud Village):** Land of Lightning. Powerful Taijutsu and Lightning style. Home of the Fourth Raikage. Attempted to kidnap Hyuga clan members in the past — caused diplomatic incident still unresolved
- **Iwagakure (Stone Village):** Land of Earth. Strong Earth-style users. Third Tsuchikage leads. Bitter history with Konoha
- **The Akatsuki:** Criminal organization of 10 S-rank Missing-nin. Secret goal: capture all nine Bijuu, extract them, and use their combined power. Current members: Pain/Nagato (Rinnegan, six bodies, leads the group), Konan, Itachi Uchiha (secretly protecting Konoha from within), Kisame Hoshigaki (shark-man, fused with sentient sword Samehada), Sasori (Puppet Master, made himself a puppet), Deidara (explosive clay), Kakuzu (has 5 hearts/lives), Hidan (immortal, death ritual cult), Zetsu (half white/half black, spy). Leader: Tobi (secretly Obito Uchiha / Madara in disguise at various points)
- **The Sannin:** Three legendary ninja of Konoha — Tsunade (medical jutsu master, slug summoner, currently gambling away her depression), Jiraiya (Toad Sage, spy network, writer of "adult" novels), Orochimaru (defected, experiments on humans to gain all jutsu and immortality — currently seeking Sasuke's body)
- **Clan Politics:** The Uchiha Clan Massacre — Itachi Uchiha killed his entire clan (except Sasuke) under secret orders from Konoha's elders (Danzo, Homura, Koharu) who feared Uchiha uprising. This secret shapes everything
- **ROOT (Foundation):** Danzo Shimura's secret black-ops Konoha division. Children conscripted, emotions removed, conditioned as weapons. Operates outside Hokage authority

**Landmarks:**
- **Konoha (The Village Hidden in the Leaves):** Founded by Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha (before their falling out). The Hokage Monument (four stone faces on the mountain). Training Ground 7 (Team 7's landmark). The Uchiha district (now abandoned after the massacre)
- **The Valley of the End:** Site of Hashirama vs Madara's legendary battle. Giant statues. Where Naruto and Sasuke have their defining conflicts
- **The Forest of Death (Training Ground 44):** Chunin Exam stage 2. 44 square km of lethal forest with giant animals
- **Orochimaru's Hideouts:** Multiple secret bases. Body experiments. Sound Village (his cover village)
- **The Land of Waves (Nami no Kuni):** Small impoverished country controlled by Gato (shipping magnate). First real mission for Team 7
- **Amegakure (Rain Village):** Pain/Nagato's base. Eternally raining. Orphans of war built a death-cult. The Akatsuki's home base
- **The Sage Mount Myoboku (Toad Mountain):** Where Toad Sage Mode is trained. Jiraiya trained here. Naruto will too
- **The Valley of Clouds and Lightning:** Where the Raikage's brother (Bee) trains with the Eight-Tails

**Lore:**
The world was shaped by chakra — the combination of physical and spiritual energy that all living things possess but few can control deliberately. The Sage of Six Paths (Hagoromo Otsutsuki) was the first user of chakra after his mother Kaguya (an alien goddess who ate the Chakra Fruit and became an immortal monster) was defeated. Hagoromo gave chakra to humanity and created Ninshu (meant to connect people) — which people weaponized into Ninjutsu. He divided the Ten-Tails (his mother's power) into nine Bijuu (Tailed Beasts) and gave them names and identities. His sons: Indra (spiritual energy, innovator, ancestor of the Uchiha) and Asura (physical energy, hardworker, ancestor of the Senju/Uzumaki). Their reincarnation war has driven the ninja world's conflicts for centuries — most recently as Madara (Indra) and Hashirama (Asura). Now: Naruto Uzumaki (Asura's latest reincarnation, Jinchuriki of Kurama the Nine-Tails) and Sasuke Uchiha (Indra's latest reincarnation, last surviving Uchiha) are children at the Ninja Academy. The Akatsuki is secretly gathering Bijuu. The Third Hokage is old. The power vacuum is coming.

**Rules of Nature / Magic System:**
- **Chakra:** Combine physical energy (stamina/body) and spiritual energy (mind/will). Shape with hand seals. Almost every ninja technique uses it
- **The Three Disciplines:** Ninjutsu (techniques using chakra — fire, water, shadow clone, summoning), Taijutsu (physical combat — Rock Lee trains ONLY this), Genjutsu (illusions — chakra interference in the opponent's senses)
- **Chakra Natures (Five):** Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, Lightning. Advanced combinations create Kekkei Genkai (bloodline limits) — Ice Release, Wood Release, Lava Release, etc.
- **Kekkei Genkai (Bloodline Limits):** Genetic traits — Sharingan (Uchiha), Byakugan (Hyuga), Rinnegan (Sage lineage), Bones (Kaguya clan), etc.
- **Sharingan → Mangekyou Sharingan → Eternal Mangekyou:** Uchiha eyes. Copy jutsu, predict movement, cast powerful Genjutsu. Mangekyou unlocked through trauma (witnessing/causing the death of someone precious). Goes blind with use. Fixed with sibling's eyes
- **Bijuu (Tailed Beasts):** 1-Tail (Shukaku, sand/wind), 2-Tails (Matatabi, fire cat), 3-Tails (Isobu, turtle), 4-Tails (Son Goku, lava), 5-Tails (Kokuo, steam), 6-Tails (Saiken, acid slug), 7-Tails (Chomei, insect), 8-Tails (Gyuki, octopus-ox), 9-Tails (Kurama, most powerful, sealed in Naruto). Jinchuriki who lose their Bijuu die
- **Summoning Contracts:** Sign in blood, summon creatures from their realm — Toads (Naruto, Jiraiya), Snakes (Orochimaru), Slugs (Tsunade, Sakura), Dogs (Kakashi)
- **Sage Mode:** Absorb natural energy. Enhances all abilities enormously. Must be perfectly balanced — too much and you turn to stone
- **Sage of Six Paths Techniques (Rinnegan):** Six Paths (Pull/Push gravitational force, summon from death, shared vision among bodies, absorption), Outer Path (reincarnate the dead — Rinne Rebirth, costs the user's life)

**Gods & Religion:**
- **Kaguya Otsutsuki:** The Rabbit Goddess. An alien princess who ate the Chakra Fruit from the God Tree on Earth. Became the first chakra user, then a monster (Ten-Tails). Sealed away. Not quite dead
- **The Sage of Six Paths (Hagoromo):** Near-divine status. Creator of modern Ninjutsu. His mother was a god, his sons founded the major bloodlines. His ghost can still manifest
- **The Bijuu:** Not worshipped but feared as forces of nature. Old cultures named them and treated them as nature deities
- **Ninja village religion:** Practical, not spiritual. The Hokage is more of a religious figure than any deity — their faces are carved into the mountain

**Languages & Cultures:**
- Universal Japanese-equivalent language
- **Ninja Culture:** The village is family. Missions are ranked (D through S). Genin (student), Chunin (mid-level), Jonin (elite), Kage (village leader). The Chunin Exams are the primary path to advancement — and a political event between villages
- **Clan Culture:** Clans within Konoha have their own traditions, techniques, and politics. Intermarriage is complex. Clan heads have significant political power
- **Missing-nin:** Ninja who defect. Hunted by their village. The most dangerous criminals. Akatsuki recruits only from them

**Economy & Resources:**
- **Mission Pay:** Ninja villages charge clients for missions. Revenue funds the village. C-rank (bodyguard), B-rank (escort dangerous), A-rank (assassination or political), S-rank (extreme danger)
- **Chakra Paper / Explosive Tags:** Standard military equipment. Crafted by specialists
- **Bijuu as Strategic Resources:** Nations with Jinchuriki have military deterrents. Losing a Bijuu = losing a nuclear weapon

**Secrets & Mysteries:**
- The Uchiha Massacre was ordered by Konoha's elders (Danzo, Homura, Koharu) with the Third Hokage's reluctant knowledge. Itachi volunteered to do it himself rather than let a war start
- Danzo (ROOT leader) has a secret arm embedded with Sharingan eyes stolen from Uchiha clan members — and Wood Release cells from Hashirama Senju grafted to his body
- Tobi (Akatsuki member acting childish and silly) is actually Obito Uchiha — a Konoha ninja believed dead, survivor of the Kannabi Bridge mission, half-body fused with Zetsu
- The Otsutsuki clan (Kaguya's people) is still out there in space — and will eventually come to harvest the chakra fruit their ancestor planted on Earth
- The true history of the Sage of Six Paths and Kaguya was deliberately erased from shinobi history to prevent anyone from seeking that level of power

**Tone & Themes:** Coming of age, found family, the cycle of hatred, what it means to be acknowledged, sacrifice, the weight of expectations, loneliness vs connection. Genuinely optimistic at its core — Naruto's answer to hatred is never to be better at hating back.

**Player Entry Point:** You are a newly graduated Genin in the Hidden Leaf. Team assignments are happening. Naruto Uzumaki is in your graduating class (or a class ahead). Sasuke is the genius everyone compares you to. The Chunin Exams are coming. The Akatsuki is moving. And somewhere, Orochimaru is watching the strong. What kind of shinobi are you, and which village claimed you?
