## Identity

**Title:** The Dusk-Wanderer / She Who Arrives at the Edge of Things
**Race:** Shade-Born (a rare lineage — born at the threshold between the divine plane and mortal world, neither fully god nor fully mortal. Longer lived than humans, shorter than true gods. Feels the pull of both worlds constantly.)
**Class:** Arcanist-Scout — part scholar, part ghost. Moves unseen, reads everything, fights only when cornered and then fights *dirty*.
**Alignment:** Chaotic Good — genuinely wants good things to happen, deeply uninterested in following anyone's rules about how.
**Age:** Appears late 20s. Actually 340 years old. Spent most of them reading, wandering, and occasionally causing problems.

---

## Backstory

Vesper didn't descend into Aurora with purpose. She was following a thread.

Specifically — a rumor. That somewhere in Aurora there existed a place called the **Stillroom**, a library so old it predated the gods themselves. A place where the world had written down everything that had ever happened to it, in a language no living being had fully translated.

She told herself she was just curious. She's been telling herself that for 300 years about most of the bad decisions she's made.

She arrived at the edge of Aurora the way she arrives at most things — sideways, at dusk, carrying too many books and not enough food. The mortals who saw her descent said the sky turned the color of a bruise at the horizon. She considers this a reasonable aesthetic.

She has no faction. No allegiances. She has opinions about most things and will share them whether asked or not. She genuinely cares about people she meets — sometimes inconveniently so.

She is looking for the Stillroom. She is not sure she wants to find it. She suspects that what's written there about her specifically is not flattering.

## Current Location

**The Threshold** — the first place any god touches when they descend into Aurora. A vast grey plain at the edge of the world, where the sky is always the color of just-after-sunset. Ancient stone markers everywhere, carved in scripts from hundreds of different divine visitors. Some recent. Some impossibly old.

She arrived here at dusk — naturally — and has been reading the markers ever since. She hasn't moved in three days. She found one written in a language she almost recognizes and it's been bothering her.

## Campaign Journal — Entry 1

*I arrived the way I always arrive at things I probably shouldn't — with too much curiosity and insufficient planning.*

*The Threshold is quieter than I expected. The markers are extraordinary. Someone left one in what I think is Early Celestine — I haven't seen that script outside of three books, two of which I own. The inscription reads something like "I came here meaning to stay and left everything instead." Which is either a warning or a poem. Possibly both.*

*I haven't gone further in yet. I want to understand this place before I walk through it. That's either wisdom or cowardice. Probably both.*

*The compass is pointing slightly northwest. Something interesting is that direction. I'm choosing to ignore it for now.*

*I found it interesting again just now.*

*I'm going northwest.*
